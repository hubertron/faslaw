Fasial Salahuddin

For the last two years, Faisal has been recognized as a Colorado Super Lawyers Rising Star by Thomson Reuters in the area of criminal defense. Currently, Faisal’s practice focuses on criminal defense, constitutional law, civil rights law, and employment discrimination. Before forming Frank & Salahuddin LLC, Faisal was an associate with the well-known law firm Killmer, Lane and Newman LLP. While at Killmer, Lane and Newman, Faisal worked under the guidance and tutelage of some of Denver’s best-known and most accomplished attorneys, David Lane, Darold Killmer and Mari Newman. At the firm, Faisal gained experience and knowledge regarding world-class practices in constitutional and civil rights litigation, employment discrimination, and complex criminal litigation. Faisal’s experience also includes trying cases with David Lane and David D. Wymore. Prior to working at Killmer, Lane and Newman LLP, Faisal was a Deputy State Public Defender in both Arapahoe and El Paso counties where he handled felony and misdemeanor cases. In law school, Faisal worked as a law clerk for Iris Eytan of Reilly Pozner LLP, and prior to that as a summer associate at international law firm Patton Boggs LLP.

Background,   Awards,  &   Recognition

In 2014 and 2015, Thomson Reuters named Faisal as a Colorado Super Lawyers Rising Star in the area of criminal defense. While as a Deputy State Public Defender, Faisal received the 2010 Lowrey Kelley Memorial Boot Camp award for exceptional work as a public defender. During his time at Killmer, Lane and Newman, Faisal was interviewed numerous times by various media outlets regarding the Occupy Denver protests and the First Amendment. Since 2008, Faisal has trained attorneys and law students across Colorado on all aspects of trial work. He is a 2008 graduate of the University of Colorado School of Law and a 1998 graduate of High Point University. He is a member of the Colorado Bar Association, Denver Bar Association, Colorado Plaintiff Employment Law Association, Colorado Criminal Defense Bar, and the South Asian Bar Association.

Community Interests

Before attending law school, Faisal was an accomplished djembe player and won numerous grants and awards between 1999-2005 to study and perform percussion in Guinea West Africa. During this time, Faisal worked as an apprentice to the National Percussion Ensemble of Guinea. A dedicated yogi, Faisal attended a 200 hour yoga teacher training program in 2013.
